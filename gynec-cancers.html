Gynaecologic Cancers

Includes cancers of ovary, cervix and uterus. Treatment planning is individualized and multidisciplinary.

When to consult

Postmenopausal bleeding, persistent abnormal bleeding or discharge, pelvic mass, abdominal distension, ascites, persistent pelvic pain, or biopsy/imaging suggesting malignancy.

Typical evaluation

Pelvic imaging (USG/CT/MRI), tumor markers when indicated, biopsy procedures and stage work-up.

Treatment approach

Depending on cancer type and stage: surgery, chemotherapy, radiation or combined treatment in a multidisciplinary setting.
